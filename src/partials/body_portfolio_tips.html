Crafting a portfolio that shows care and consideration is paramount, especially so if you’re new to the field and on the lookout for a new role. There are a litany of completely avoidable mistakes that designers need to keep an eye out for as they build up their portfolio. Our friends at Designlab joined us to outline the most common mistakes junior UX designers make when crafting their portfolios—and how they can be avoided. Some of the go-to methods for maneuvering around common portfolio pitfalls involve remembering to:

  • Never forget the user

  • Show the right amount of work

  • Show the right amount of narrative

  • Provide clarity about your role

  • Show optimized images

  • Have perfect copy

  • Feature a call-to-action

Before starting any prep work on your own refresh, be sure to check out our complete article for Designlab’s step-by-step tips to build your UX portfolio in a way that will make you stand out and shine. Also remember Designlab’s take on the importance of showing your work off well:

“A portfolio site is a place to display your work online. But if you’ve ever tried updating your design portfolio, you know that the decisions involved can quickly get overwhelming. What’s more, the stakes are high—get those decisions right, and they can land you a job. Get them wrong, and you might be passed by.” ■
